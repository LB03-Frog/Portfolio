Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

I believe that the act of making, specifically in modern times, is strongly linked to rhetoric, technology, and ethics. When we create things in this class we will be using many different technologies as a medium for making. We will creating things to share our opinion with others and learning how to do that most effectivly. To ensure our designs are etheical, we will be adding accessibility features to our designs to ensure everyone can experiance our designs.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Design principles provide a foundation for creating meaningful and professional work. By applying concepts such as balance, contrast, alignment, proximity, and repetition, we learn to shape both the message and the visual impact of a project. These principles give us a shared vocabulary for design that helps us strengthen our ideas and communicate more clearly with different audiences.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Workshops emphasize collaboration and respect, helping us improve by listening to others and offering constructive feedback. By participating actively, we learn how our peers interpret our projects and how we can support their growth in return. This mutual process of critique and revision builds trust and sharpens our ability to refine ideas through dialogue.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Revision is essential in moving from small ideas to larger, more polished projects. In this course we will practice scaling our work, translating designs across multiple media, and experimenting with how form affects meaning. These revisions allow us to push our projects further, ensuring that what we make develops into clear, effective, and engaging communication.

5. Learn to present your work.

Presentation requires us to explain not only what we made but also why and how we made it. Sharing the theory, method, and design choices behind a project helps audiences understand our work and connect with it on a deeper level. Through oral presentations and portfolio-building, we will practice communicating both the process and the purpose of our designs.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

As we learned in the accessibility project the most important things when creating ethical designs is to ensure everyone can understand what we create. Through specific methods we can ensure that viewers can interact with the design regardless of their disability. For example we can ensure the text has a high contrast compared to the colors below it. We can also create smaller text boxes and embed an order into the box.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Design principles provide a foundation for creating meaningful and professional work. In my work I use strong contrast and intentional layouts to increase readability and improve the designs. I select colors that work well together but can also stand out. This ensures my designs stand out and can be understood in a variety of different settings and from a variety of different perspectives

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Workshops emphasize collaboration and respect, helping us improve by listening to others and offering constructive feedback. In our logo design project we leveraged the feedback of others to finalize a logo design. First We created an array of testers that are unique and designed around different principles. Next we tested them with reviewers to gain feedback on what worked and what was unsuccessful. We then used this feedback to create an improved final design. These steps ensure the final design is an improvement to the original iterations.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

By submitting this portfolio multiple times we are able to edit and revise our work, this ensures our final submission is more effective. These revisions allow us to push our projects further, ensuring that what we make develops into clear, effective, and engaging communication.

5. Learn to present your work.

Presentation requires us to explain not only what we made but also why and how we made it. Sharing the theory, method, and design choices behind a project helps audiences understand our work and connect with it on a deeper level. We improve our presentation skills through small group work. This helps develop our skills without the pressure of presenting in front of a large audience.
